<p><p>Mid and high-level precision GPS receivers are categorized based on their frequency capabilities. Single-frequency receivers operate on one frequency band, typically providing basic positioning accuracy, suitable for consumer applications. In contrast, dual-frequency receivers utilize two frequency bands, which enhances accuracy by mitigating errors from atmospheric conditions and multipath effects. This makes dual-frequency GPS devices ideal for professional and industrial applications, such as surveying and agriculture, where precise location data is critical for operations.</p></p>
