<p><p>Level sensors designed for the hygienic market are categorized into two main types: contact and non-contact. Contact type sensors, which include capacitive and ultrasonic models, directly interact with the material, ensuring accurate measurement while maintaining hygiene standards. Non-contact type sensors, such as radar and laser systems, measure levels without physical contact, reducing contamination risk. Both types are essential for industries like food and pharmaceuticals, where cleanliness and precise monitoring are crucial for quality and safety.</p></p>
