<p><p>The multi-axial fabric market is segmented based on the orientation of its fibers. Uniaxial fabric features fibers aligned in one direction, providing strength primarily along that axis. Biaxial fabric incorporates fibers in two perpendicular directions, enhancing strength and stability. Triaxial fabric has fibers arranged in three directions, offering balanced mechanical properties. Quadaxial fabric consists of fibers oriented in four axes, maximizing strength and resistance. Other fabrics may include various hybrid designs tailored for specific applications or performance requirements.</p></p>
