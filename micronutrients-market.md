<p><p>The micronutrients market is categorized into three primary types: powder, granule, and liquid forms. Powder micronutrients are finely ground for easy mixing and application, often used in fertilizers. Granules provide a slow-release option, enhancing nutrient availability over time in soil. Liquid micronutrients are typically more concentrated and allow for faster absorption by plants, making them ideal for foliar applications. Each form has unique advantages, catering to different agricultural needs and cultivation methods while ensuring optimal plant health and growth.</p></p>
