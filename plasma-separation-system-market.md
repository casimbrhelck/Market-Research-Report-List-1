<p><p>The Plasma Separation System market features two primary types: membrane separation and centrifugal separation. Membrane separation utilizes selective filters to separate plasma components based on size and permeability, offering advantages like energy efficiency and compact design. In contrast, centrifugal separation employs high-speed rotation to generate forces that separate plasma from blood cells, yielding high purity and efficiency. Each method caters to different operational needs, with varying costs and processing capabilities in medical and biopharmaceutical applications.</p></p>
