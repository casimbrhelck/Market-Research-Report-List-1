<p><p>The automotive image recognition camera market is primarily categorized into 2-D and 3-D cameras. 2-D cameras capture flat images, enabling essential functions like lane departure warnings and obstacle detection. They rely on high-resolution imaging for effective analysis. In contrast, 3-D cameras utilize depth perception and stereoscopic imaging, providing a more comprehensive understanding of the vehicle's environment. This advanced capability enhances features such as pedestrian detection and automated driving, making 3-D cameras increasingly vital for modern automotive safety and automation systems.</p></p>
