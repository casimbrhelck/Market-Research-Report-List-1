<p><p>The medical grade adhesive market is divided into two main types: natural and synthetic adhesives. Natural adhesives, derived from organic materials, offer biocompatibility and are often used in wound dressings and surgical applications. Synthetic adhesives, on the other hand, are chemically formulated for enhanced strength and durability, making them suitable for a variety of medical uses, including device fixation and tissue bonding. Both types cater to diverse needs, ensuring safety and efficacy in medical procedures while addressing varying performance requirements.</p></p>
