<p><p>The Hollow Core Insulator market is primarily categorized into two types: ceramic and composite insulators. Ceramic insulators are made from materials like porcelain, known for their high dielectric strength and durability, making them suitable for high-voltage applications. Composite insulators, on the other hand, utilize polymeric materials combined with glass or fiberglass, offering lightweight characteristics and improved resistance to environmental factors. Both types serve critical roles in electrical systems, ensuring reliable performance and safety in power transmission and distribution.</p></p>
